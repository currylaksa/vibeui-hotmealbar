# Hot Meal Bar — Domain Glossary

The shared language for this project. Definitions only — no implementation details.

## Hot Meal Bar
The restaurant. A Halal Chinese-Muslim eatery (Arabic: مطعم المسلم الصيني)
cooking in a Northwestern-Chinese / Xinjiang / Hui style. Fully halal: no pork,
no lard. Audience: students, staff, families, and local foodies.

## Mee Tarik
The hero dish — noodles pulled by hand to order ("tarik" = pulled). Reviewed as
the "best mee tarik in Bangi". The single most important item on the site.

## Beef Soup Noodles
Signature noodle dish whose praised hero is the broth. Customarily served with
chili oil offered separately.

## Chili Oil
The house signature condiment — special, on the spicy side. Doubles as the
brand's recurring visual motif across the site (the "chili-oil drip").

## Dumplings
Chicken, beef-carrot, and fried varieties. Sold in quantity (≈25 pcs for RM10).

## Ox Soup
A bowl of beef/ox soup, ≈RM8.

## Menu Rahmah
A value meal at RM5: pick one vegetable + one protein. "Rahmah" (Malay/Arabic,
"mercy/compassion") signals affordability for the community.

## Branch
A physical Hot Meal Bar location. All three are open: UKM Bangi (PUSANIKA,
Level 3), Cyberjaya, and UTM Johor Bahru (the newest table, treated as a
"now open" highlight).

## Value Props
The brand's affordability promises: student discount (with student card),
free drink refills, generous portions, and the fully-halal positioning.

## Dakwah Framing
Some Chinese-Muslim owners frame their food as culinary dakwah (outreach);
heritage and the Hui/Xinjiang Silk-Road story are part of the brand identity.
